# Playbook: Payment Issues

## Covers These Intents
- "I didn't get paid"
- "My payment is wrong"
- "Where's my money?"
- "How do I set up instant pay?"
- "Payment sent to wrong account"
- "I was flagged for fraud"

## Missing Payment — First Response
Check these before escalating:
1. Is the shift showing as "Completed" in the app?
2. Is direct deposit / Stripe set up correctly?
3. Has it been less than 48 hours? (manual verification takes up to 48 hrs)

Response: "Let me help troubleshoot. Can you check if the shift shows as 'Completed' in your app and that your direct deposit is set up under Settings > Payment? If both look good and it's been more than 48 hours, I'll submit a support ticket for the payments team."

## Instant Pay Issues
- Daily limit: one withdrawal per day (resets midnight PST)
- New cards: ineligible for 7 days
- $1.99 fee per transaction
- Once Standard payout initiated, can't switch to Instant

Response: "Instant Pay lets you withdraw once per day. If you just added a new card, there's a 7-day wait for security. Each withdrawal has a $1.99 fee. If you're seeing an error, try again tomorrow after midnight PST."

## Payment to Wrong Account
Cannot reverse once initiated. Partner must contact their bank.

Response: "Unfortunately, once a transfer starts we can't reverse it. If it went to the wrong account, contact your bank to move the funds. If the transfer fails, your funds will reset and you can withdraw to a different card."

## Flagged for Fraud
Flagged ≠ fraud confirmed. Just means extra review.

Response: "Having a shift flagged just means it needs extra review — it doesn't mean anything is wrong. This usually takes 24-72 hours. To avoid flags in the future, make sure to stay on site for your full shift and complete 100% of your task list."

## Partner Frustrated About Payment
If partner is sarcastic or frustrated ("just keep the money"), do NOT close the conversation. Offer investigation.

Response: "I understand the frustration. Let me submit a support ticket for a payment investigation to make sure you're compensated fairly. Can you confirm the shift date and store?"

## Escalation
- Missing payment after 48 hours → escalate
- Payment amount disputed → need shift date, store number, expected vs received amount
- Partner claims they were promised specific payment → escalate, do not confirm or deny
