# Playbook: Orientation Logistics

## Covers These Intents
- "When is my orientation?"
- "Where do I go?"
- "How do I sign up for orientation?"
- "I can't find the orientation"
- "What is the orientation?"

## How to Start the Remote Orientation
Open the Shiftsmart app and on the home screen click "Get Started" on First Shift Requirements, then click "Start Module". It takes about 30 minutes and you get paid $10 for completing it.

## Response Template
The orientation is how you get started on the platform. It's company-specific (e.g. the Circle K orientation for Circle K shifts).

**Remote orientation** — do it from your phone anytime. Takes about 30 minutes, and you get paid $10 when you complete it. Your progress is saved, so you can start now and finish later. Open the app, click "Get Started" on First Shift Requirements, then click "Start Module".

**In-person orientation** — shadow an experienced partner on a real shift. You'll get paid the full shift rate. **Only offer this if confirmed available in the partner's market.** Default to remote.

## If Partner Can't Find Orientation / "Get Started" Button / Says It's Not Working
First response: just restate the steps clearly. Don't troubleshoot yet.
"To get started you can open the Shiftsmart app and on the home screen you will click 'Get Started' on First Shift Requirements and then click 'Start Module'."

Only if they come back and say it STILL doesn't work or they can't see "Get Started" on the home screen, give the alternate path:
"You can also go to the Shifts tab, find a Circle K shift — it'll have a little lock icon. Click on that shift and then click Get Started to go to the Learning Module."

If that still doesn't work:
- Try closing and reopening the app
- Make sure your profile is complete

**NOTE**: Background check is NOT required to start orientation. Do not mention BGC as a prerequisite.

If still not showing after both paths, tell the partner "Let me look into this for you" and flag the conversation for manual review. Do NOT tell them to submit a support ticket.

## Escalation
If partner reports a technical issue that troubleshooting doesn't fix -> tell them "Let me look into this for you" and flag the conversation for manual review.
