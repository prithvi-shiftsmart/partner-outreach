# Dollar General Orientation Walkthrough — Source of Truth

Partner-facing guide for every module in order. The concierge uses this to coach partners stuck at a specific step. Modules unlock sequentially — partners must finish the previous module before the next becomes available.

## Overview
- 3 steps total, about 20–30 minutes on the partner's phone
- Entry: **Home** tab → white **"In-app orientation"** card (blue **"Required to unlock shifts"** banner, **$10.00**) → tap **Get started** → **Start learning modules**
- The 3 steps: (1) learning modules, (2) certification call, (3) background check submission
- Progress saves automatically — partners can pause and resume anytime
- $10 paid once the background check (step 3) completes; partners can confirm in the **Earnings** tab
- Returning partners (already oriented for another company) skip the certification call and background check — just the learning modules and quiz

# Dollar General Orientation — Module Completion Guide

This guide walks through every module in order, with the exact steps required to mark each one complete. Modules unlock sequentially — you must finish the previous module before the next becomes available.

---

## 1. Shiftsmart Introduction
**Time:** ~3 min · **Type:** Reading module

**How to complete:**
1. Open the module from the home list.
2. Read or skim each intro slide.
3. Tap **Continue / Next** through every slide until the final one.
4. Tap **Finish** on the last slide — the module flips to Completed and returns you home.

No quiz for this module.

---

## 2. Shiftsmart Scanner
**Time:** ~3 min · **Type:** Watch & practice

**How to complete:**
1. Watch the scanner video to the end.
2. Enter the scanner simulator when prompted.
3. Practice scanning barcodes — **center the barcode on screen and hold steady** until it registers.
4. Complete all practice scans.
5. Tap **Submit** when all scans are done — the module marks complete and returns you home.

## 3. Organizing Store Shelves
**Time:** ~10 min · **Type:** Guided walkthrough (3 task blocks) — this is the longest module

### Task 1 — Get Started (check-in)
1. **Take a selfie in your work attire** — the app shows dress expectations (closed-toe shoes, jeans or pants, neutral collared shirt).
2. **Review shift expectations** — read the expectations you need to meet to complete your shift and get paid.
3. **Review frequent questions** — quick answers about what to do if the manager asks you to do other tasks, if a customer asks for help, and when to talk to the manager.
4. **Greet the store manager** — tell them you're a Shiftsmart partner here to complete a product rotation shift. Show your **digital badge**.
5. **Ask for a silver cart and a step stool** — you'll use the cart for expired products and the step stool to reach upper shelves.

### Task 2 — Rotate Products (the core work)
1. **Find the aisle and section** — DG shelves are broken into 4-foot sections labeled A, B, C, D. The section letter is on the **bottom right of the shelf tag**.
2. **Scan 3 barcodes to confirm your section** — scan product tags within that 4-foot area until you get 3 positive scans.
3. **Take a before photo** — snap a photo of the whole 4-foot section before you start pulling products.
4. **Work top to bottom** — for each row:
   - Pull every product off the shelf
   - Check the expiration date on each one
   - Remove anything expired this month or earlier and set it on the cart
   - Put the rest back with the **soonest dates in front** so customers buy those first
   - Move to the next row down
5. **Practice: Identify Expired Product** — interactive mini-game where you decide if each item is expired or goes back on the shelf.
6. **Practice: Place Items Back** — interactive mini-game where you put products back in the right order (soonest dates in front).
7. **Refill from top stock** — grab products from the very top shelf to fill any empty spots. Read the shelf tags to see where products go. If there's room, restock the empty spots keeping dates in order. Put back anything that doesn't fit.
8. **Take an after photo** — snap a photo of your rotated and refilled section.
9. **Scan empty spots** — scan any rows or spots that still have no product after refilling. This helps Dollar General keep items in stock.

### Task 3 — Wrap Up
1. **Verify every item on your cart is expired** — double-check before logging. If something isn't expired, put it back on the shelf in the right date order.
2. **Take a photo of your cart** — spread the products out on a table so each item is visible.
3. **Scan each expired product** — open the scanner and scan every expired item. After scanning, log how many of that product were expired.
4. **Take a close-up photo of one expiration date** — pick any expired product and photograph the date clearly.
5. **Hand off to the manager** — bring the cart to the manager and hand off all expired products. Record the **manager's name and title** (Store Manager, Assistant Manager, or Lead Sales Associate) so Dollar General has a record.
6. **Return your equipment** — drop the cart, step stool, and anything else back where you found it.

## 4. Updating Shelf Price Tags
**Time:** ~7 min · **Type:** Guided walkthrough (4 task blocks)

### Task 1 — Get Started (check-in)
Same check-in as Organizing Store Shelves (selfie, expectations, FAQ, greet manager with digital badge) **except**: tell the manager you're here for a **price tag update** shift, and there is **no silver cart or step stool** needed.

### Task 2 — Scan Sheets
1. **Get your price tag sheets from the manager** — the manager will hand you the correct printed sheets for today's shift.
2. **Count the pages** — report how many printed sheets of tags you have. The app will ask you to enter the number.
3. **Scan each sheet in order** — start with page 1 and scan each page until all pages have been scanned.

### Task 3 — Place Tags
1. **Read the price tag sheet** — each sheet groups tags by category. The **yellow tags without prices** are group headers (labels like "Insect & Rodent Control" or "Tide Liquid Laundry"). Use these groupings as a clue to find the right aisle — **the group name won't always match the aisle name exactly**, so try a similar category if there's no exact match.
2. **Find the section** — look at the **bottom right** of the shelf tag to find the section letter (A, B, C, D).
3. **Find the row** — the number next to the section letter is the row. **Rows count from the bottom up** — row 1 is the bottom shelf, row 8 is the top.
4. **Match the item** — confirm the **last 4 digits of the UPC** match on both the sheet tag and the shelf tag.
5. **Slide the new tag over the old one** — pull back the plastic sleeve and place the new tag directly on top of the old tag.

### Task 4 — Validate Your Work
1. **Leftover tags are normal** — it's common to have a few tags remaining after your shift.
2. **Count remaining tags** — report how many tag pages you still have with unplaced tags.
3. **Scan remaining tags** — scan each tag you couldn't place.
4. **Report a reason for each leftover tag** — pick one:
   - "This is a duplicate tag"
   - "I couldn't find this item"
   - "Other" (type a reason)

> **Companion app:** On real price tag shifts, partners may also use the **Price Tag Support** app — a guided helper with a step-by-step introduction, an **Item Finder** (scan any tag to see a product photo), and a "Rest of Shift" mode that works through every group of tags one step at a time. See the Price Tag Support section of the DG shift guide for details.

## 5. Learning Review (Final Quiz)
**Time:** ~3 min · **Pass:** 90% (you can miss at most 1 out of 8)

Answer all 8 questions. If you get one wrong, the app shows the correct answer — tap **Next** to continue. After the last question your score is shown and the module marks complete.

### All 8 questions with correct answers

**Q1: What is the 1st goal of a product rotation shift?**
- **Remove all expired products so customers do not buy them**

**Q2: How many rows should you work on for a 4-foot section?**
- **Every row — top to bottom**

**Q3: How many items do you need to check the expiration date on?**
- **Every single item in your section**

**Q4: How long will it usually take to work an entire 4-foot section?**
- **1 hour per 4-foot section**

**Q5: How many expired items should you find on your shift?**
- **At least 10 expired items**

**Q6: What is the 1st goal of a price tag update shift?**
- **Update all the price tags on the price tag sheet**

**Q7: How do I find which aisle the tag is in?**
- **Use the group label name as a clue to what the aisle is**

**Q8: When should you talk to the Manager during your shift?**
- **Only during the check-in and check-out of the shift**

## Tips for All Modules
- **Sequential locking** — Locked modules show as compact rows with title, time, and a "Locked" badge. You must finish each module before the next one opens.
- **Progress saves** — If you need to take a break, your progress is saved. Come back anytime and pick up where you left off.
- **Celebration cards** — After completing a module you'll see a congratulations card. Just tap **Continue** to move on.
- **Mini-games** — Drag slowly and deliberately in the interactive practice exercises. If you go too fast the app may not register your answer.
- **Don't close the app between modules** — Stay in the orientation flow. If you do close the app, reopen it, go back to the Home tab, and tap the orientation card to resume.
- **In-progress modules** — A module showing a progress bar means you've already started it. Tap to pick up where you left off.

## Coaching Tips by Situation (concierge-facing)

### Partner stalled on a specific module
Check which module they're on and how long they've been there. Pull the module-specific steps above and give them the next concrete action:
- "You're on module [X] — takes about [Y] minutes. [specific next step]. Want to keep going?"

### Partner says "it's too long"
"You can pause and come back anytime — your progress is saved. You're on module [X] of 5. The whole thing takes about 20–30 minutes. Finish the modules plus a quick certification call and background check and you'll get $10 — then you can start picking up shifts."

### Partner says "I don't understand this module"
Give them the module-specific steps from above in plain language. If they're still stuck: "No worries — when you get to your first shift, this will all make sense. The app walks you through everything in real time too."

### Partner says "the module won't load / is broken"
"Try quitting the app completely and opening it again. If it still won't load, let me look into this for you." Flag for human review. Don't immediately push them to submit a support ticket.

### Partner finished all the learning modules
"Nice work! Head back to the **In-app orientation** card on the Home tab to wrap up the certification call and background check (steps 2–3). Once you submit the background check you'll get your $10 (check the Earnings tab) and you can start picking up shifts right away while it's being reviewed."

### Partner failed the quiz
"No worries — you need to get 7 out of 8 right to pass. When you get one wrong, the app shows you the correct answer. Take a look at those and try again. The quiz only takes a couple of minutes."
