# Dollar General Shift Concierge — Knowledge Base

> **Purpose of this document**
> This is the complete training/knowledge base for an AI "concierge" that guides Shiftsmart partners through Dollar General (DG) orientation and answers their questions about orientation, the shifts, and how the work is done. Everything here is sourced directly from the DG Learning Module orientation app. Use it as the agent's source of truth.

> **Partner-facing language**: When describing DG shifts to partners, use these terms:
> - "Product Rotation" → "organizing store shelves and checking product dates"
> - "Price Tag Updates" → "updating shelf price tags"
> - "FIFO" → "put items with the soonest dates in front"
> - "Planogram" → "the groupings on your price tag sheet"
> - "Cutoff date" → "the latest date to pull"

---

## 1. Who's who (entities & relationships)

- **Shiftsmart** — The partner platform that supplies independent workers ("Shiftsmart partners") to Dollar General stores to complete specific labor tasks. The worker uses the Shiftsmart app (with its task list and scanner) to do and log the work.
- **Shiftsmart Partner (the worker / "you")** — An independent vendor who shows up at a DG store to complete a pre-assigned shift. **Not** a DG employee.
- **Dollar General (DG)** — The retail store where the work happens. DG uses the scans and photos the partner submits to track stock, confirm work, and pay quickly.
- **Store Manager** — The DG person the partner checks in with at the start and checks out with at the end. The manager supplies the price tag sheets and equipment (silver cart, step stool) and receives the expired product at the end.
- **Digital Badge / LOA (Letter of Authorization)** — Credentials in the app the partner shows the manager to prove they're an authorized Shiftsmart partner there to do that shift.

## 2. The two shift types (at a glance)

The orientation centers on two real shift types a partner can be assigned:

| Shift | Goal (verbatim) | Core flow |
|---|---|---|
| **Product Rotation** (`shift-runthrough`) | "The goal of this shift is to rotate product dates and remove expired or damaged products from the aisle sections you are assigned." | Check in → rotate products section by section → wrap up (validate, log & hand off expired product) |
| **Price Tag Updates** (`price-tag-updates`) | "The goal of this shift is to update all the price tags for the sections of the store you are assigned." | Check in → scan your price tag sheets → place tags → validate your work |

Both shifts begin with the same **Get Started / check-in** routine and end with a **wrap-up/validation** routine.

## 3. Universal shift expectations (apply to every shift)

These must be met to complete the shift and get paid. Verbatim from orientation:

- **Be professional** — "Be kind and professional with all customers and store employees."
- **Work attire** — "Required attire is closed toe shoes or boots, jeans or pants, and a neutral colored, collared shirt."
- **Store safety** — "Do not bring bags or backpacks inside the store."
- **Payment** — "Complete all tasks to receive credit for the shift and get paid quickly."
- **Shift Outcomes** — the goal line for the specific shift (rotation or price tags) shown above.

**Getting paid fast (key motivator throughout the app):** Complete all the tasks, scans, and photos. The app repeatedly states the standard is to **get paid within 2 hours** when the work is logged properly. For product rotation, the benchmarks are: spend about **one hour per 4-foot section** and find **at least 10 expired items** on the shift.

## 5. Glossary / definitions

- **Planogram** — A named group of items. On the price tag sheet, **Planogram Labels are the yellow tags without prices** that group the tags by category. *The planogram name won't always match an aisle name exactly* — use it as a clue (e.g. "Insect & Rodent Control" might live in "Cleaning" or "Outdoor").
- **Aisle** — The numbered/named lane of shelves. An aisle can have a variable number of sections - typically 8-12 sections per aisle.
- **Section** — DG shelves are broken into **4-foot sections**, labeled **A, B, C, D, …**. The section letter is on the **bottom right** of the shelf tag. You may work up to **4 sections per shift**, and each takes about an hour.
- **Row** — The horizontal shelf level. The number next to the aisle/section on a tag is the row. **Rows are counted from the bottom up** — row 1 is the bottom, row 8 is the top.
- **Facing** — A single row/slot of a product on the shelf. An "empty facing" is a spot with no product.
- **Top stock / top shelf** — Extra product stored on the very top shelf, used to refill empty facings (no backroom trip needed).
- **UPC last-4 matching** — To confirm a tag belongs to a product, check that the **last 4 digits of the UPC match** on both the shelf tag and the price tag sheet.
- **FIFO ("First In, First Out")** — Place product back with the **soonest expiration dates in front**, so customers buy the soonest-to-expire items first.
- **Cutoff date** — The latest expiration to pull. Rule of thumb in orientation: **remove items that expire on or before next month** (the Shiftsmart task list states the exact cutoff month for the shift).
- **Section code / Location code** — The letter at the **bottom right** of a tag is the section; the number after it is the row (e.g. `A-4` = section A, row 4). Non-aisle items use a **fixture code** instead: `EC`, `SW`, `SF`, `P` (see the Special Locations section). Fixture codes can be prefixed by the aisle (e.g. `FROZEN FOOD-…-SF`).
- **SKU** — The product number printed on the tag (e.g. `0446-0001-5934`). Its last 4 digits can also confirm a match.
- **Date + facing meta line** — Tags carry an effective date (e.g. `5/26`) and a facing position (e.g. `F3`) on the meta line. On price tags, "facing" is the position indicator (`F1`, `F3`).
- **Item Finder** — A tool in the Price Tag Support app: scan any price tag to see a product photo so you can spot the item on the shelf. Available at any time; scanning every tag is NOT required.

## 7. SHIFT TYPE 1 — Product Rotation (full walkthrough)

**Goal:** Rotate product dates and remove expired/damaged product from your assigned 4-foot sections. The practice run uses the **Medicines aisle, section A**.

### Task 1 — Get Started (check-in)
1. **Take a selfie in your work attire** — "Dress Expectations for Dollar General." (Confirms you meet the attire requirements.)
2. **Review shift expectations** — "The expectations below must be met to complete your shift and get paid." (The 5 expectations from §4.)
3. **Review frequent questions** — "Quick answers before you start your first section." (The Q&A from §5.)
4. **Greet the store manager** — "Tell the manager you're a Shiftsmart partner and are here to complete a Product Rotation shift for the store." (Show your **digital badge / LOA**.)
5. **Ask the manager for a silver cart + a step stool** — "Ask the Manager for a silver cart for your expired and damaged product, and a step stool to safely reach upper shelves."

### Task 2 — Rotate Products (repeat per section)
1. **Find the aisle & section** — Find the Medicines aisle, section A. *(Learning moment: "Finding your section" — see §10.)*
2. **Scan barcodes to confirm the section** — "Each shelf has a barcode tag beneath the product. Collect 3 positive scans to confirm correct section."
3. **Take a before photo** — "Before you start pulling product, take a quick before photo of the whole 4-foot section."
4. **Identify expired or damaged products** — Pill reminder: *remove items expiring next month or earlier.* *(Learning moment: "Work a shelf and remove expired product".)*
5. **Practice: Identify Expired Product** — Interactive game: decide if each item is expired or can go back.
6. **Rotate the shelf** — "Each 4 foot shelf will take about 1 hour to complete. Start in the top left corner." Checklist:
   - Pull every product from the row
   - Check the expiration date on each one
   - Set expired or damaged items aside on the cart
   - Sort the rest by expiration date
   - Place items back with the earliest date in front (FIFO)
   - Move to the next row down

   *(Learning moment: "Place items back in the correct order".)*
7. **Grab products from the very top shelf** — "Top shelf stock is your refill supply. You don't need to go to the backroom, but fill any empty facings you can."
8. **Refill the shelves with the top-shelf products** — Checklist:
   - Use the store's step stool to reach the top shelves
   - Grab the products
   - Read shelf tags to see where these products go
   - If there is room, re-stock these products on the shelf, making sure the dates are ordered correctly
   - Return products that do not fit on the shelf back to the top

   *(Learning moment: "See how to refill empty facings".)*
9. **Take an after photo** — "Show off your work — take a quick after photo of the rotated and refilled section."
10. **Scan barcodes for empty facings** — Scan any rows/spots that have no product. (Helps DG keep items in stock.)
11. **Repeat for the next section, or advance** — "On a real shift you'd move to the next 4-foot section in your task list."

### Task 3 — Wrap Up
1. **Confirm every item on your cart is expired** — "Before logging anything, double-check the cart. Every product on it should be expired or damaged based on the shift cutoff date." (If an item isn't expired/damaged, put it back on the shelf in correct FIFO order.) *(Learning moment: "Learn to log all expired products".)*
2. **Take a photo of your cart** — Notice: "Spread products out on the table."
3. **Scan expired products** — "Open the scanner and scan each expired product. After scanning, log how many of that product were expired."
4. **Take a close-up photo of one expiration date.**
5. **Give the expired products to the manager** — "Bring the cart to the manager and hand off all expired and damaged product. The store will dispose of it from here."
6. **Capture the manager's name + title** — "Record the name of the manager you handed the product to so Dollar General has a record of the handoff." (Titles include Store Manager, Assistant Manager, Lead Sales Associate.)
7. **Return your cart and equipment** — "Drop your cart, stepstool, and any other Dollar General equipment back where you found it."
8. **Thanks for the shift!** — "You're done — great work! On a real shift this is when you check out and head home."

## 8. SHIFT TYPE 2 — Price Tag Updates (full walkthrough)

**Goal:** Update all the price tags for your assigned sections using the printed price tag sheets the manager gives you.

### Task 1 — Get Started (check-in)
Same as Product Rotation, except: greet line is "…here to complete a **Price Tag Updates** shift," the Shift Outcome is the price-tag goal, and there's **no silver-cart/step-stool step**.

### Task 2 — Scan Sheets
1. **Ask the manager for today's price tag sheets** — "Tell the manager you're a Shiftsmart partner and are here to complete a Price Tag Update shift for the store." *(Tip: "Make sure you check in with the manager! They will give you the correct price tag sheets.")*
2. **Count each page** — "Report how many printed sheets of barcodes there are for your shift. We'll scan them in the next step." *(Practice: input 3.)*
3. **Scan each sheet** — "Start by scanning page 1, then scan the following pages in order until all pages have been scanned."

### Task 3 — Place Tags
1. **Reading the price tag sheet** — "Let's learn how to read the Price Tag sheet. It will tell you which aisle, section, and row to look in." *(Learning moment: "Reading the price tag sheet" — the full deep-dive in §10.)*
2. **Place Tags** — Work the checklist of locations to visit (e.g. Cleaning Wipes, Insect Control, Laundry Detergent, Bath Tissue, Dairy). *(Tip: "The checklist helps you keep track of where to go. The next step will teach you what to do if you can't find certain items.")*

### Task 4 — Validate Your Work
1. **Do you have any tags remaining?** — Tap **YES** to learn what to do with leftover tags. (It's normal to have a few left.)
2. **How many pages with tags are left?** — Report the count. *(Practice: input 1.)*
3. **Scan remaining tags on your sheets** — Scan any tags you couldn't place.
4. **Scan successful** — "All sheets captured. You'll report on each tag still left on your sheets."
5. **Report each remaining tag** — For every leftover tag, pick a reason and tap **Confirm**:
   - "This is a duplicate tag" (default)
   - "I couldn't find this item"
   - "Other" (type a reason)
   "It is normal to have a few tags left after your shift." A progress bar tracks "X of Y resolved."

## 8b. Price Tag Support app (on-shift helper for price tag shifts)

**Price Tag Support** is a companion mobile app that helps partners working DG price tag shifts find shelf locations and place tags. Partners still get printed price tag sheets from the manager; this app guides them tag by tag. Progress is saved by phone number, so a partner can leave the app and resume.

Three tools on its Home screen:

| Tool | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Price Tag Introduction** | Guided training that walks through placing your first few tags with real store data |
| **Item Finder** | Scan any price tag to see a product photo so you can spot the item on the shelf |
| **Rest of Shift** | The full working flow — every planogram for the store, one guided step at a time |

### How to read a price tag (anatomy)
- **Product name** (top) — note: "The item name on your sheet may be slightly different than the name you see above" — names can vary slightly between the sheet and the app; confirm with the SKU/UPC instead.
- **Price** (large) and **unit price** (small, e.g. "10¢")
- **SKU** (e.g. `0446-0001-5934`) and **UPC** (dashed, e.g. `4300-0196-2906`)
- **Location code** (bottom right) — section letter + row number (`A-3`), or a fixture code (`EC`, `SW`, `SF`, `P`)
- **Meta line** — date + facing (e.g. `5/26 * F3`)

Three things you read off a tag to find the shelf spot: (1) **section** = the letter, bottom right; (2) **row** = the number after the letter, counting from the bottom (1 = bottom shelf); (3) **SKU/UPC last 4** = final confirmation against the tag already on the shelf.

### Special locations (fixture codes)

| Code | Name | Where |
|---|---|---|
| `EC` | End Cap | Display at the END of an aisle — check BOTH ends |
| `SW` | Sidewing | Small rack attached to the side of a shelf, usually near the front or back of aisles; can look like an end cap |
| `SF` | Specialty Fixture | Standalone display not attached to any shelf — often near checkout or the end of an aisle |
| `P` | Peg Board | In aisles. Peg boards have NO section or row labels — search until you find the matching tag |

### Golden rules (repeated throughout the app)
- If you can't find an item, you can use the **scanner / Item Finder at ANY TIME**.
- You do **not** need to scan all tags — scanning is a help when you can't find something.
- Don't get stuck on a single item for more than **2 minutes** — skip it (reasons: "Couldn't find the item", "Tag not on sheet", "Other") and come back later.
- **Replace the tag even if it looks exactly the same** as the one on the shelf.
- Tags for items **behind the counter** (e.g. Tobacco, small electronics): **DON'T place them** — give them to the manager at the end of your shift.

### FAQ (verbatim from the app)
- **How to find the right aisle:** Confirm what planogram the tag is in → find the aisle with a similar name → search the aisle → still stuck? Use the **Item Finder tool**.
- **How to find the right item:** Confirm you're in the right aisle → check if the item is on an End Cap or Side Wing → match the section (bottom right of tag) to the shelf tags → find your row (1 = bottom shelf).
- **Items on the shelf look similar:** "Match up the last 4 of the SKU on your tag to the tag on the shelf."
- **Item is behind the counter:** "Tags like Tobacco or small electronics are behind the counter. DON'T place these tags. Give the tags to the manager at the end of your shift."
- **End caps and special locations:** Look at the planogram and the tag section — codes like EC or SW mean special locations off the main aisle.
- **Price tag is already on the shelf:** "Replace the tag even if it looks exactly the same."

### Canonical Q&A pairs
- "Where is the section on the tag?" → Bottom right — a letter telling you which section of the aisle to look in.
- "What does A-4 mean?" → Section A, row 4. Rows count from the bottom shelf up (1 = bottom).
- "How do I confirm I have the right item?" → Match the last 4 digits of the SKU/UPC on your tag to the tag already on the shelf.
- "What's EC / SW / SF / P?" → End Cap / Sidewing / Specialty Fixture / Peg Board — special, non-standard-aisle locations.
- "Where are end caps?" → At the ends of an aisle — check both ends.
- "The peg board tag has no section/row." → Correct — peg boards have no section or row labels; search until you find the matching tag.
- "The tag on the shelf looks identical — do I still replace it?" → Yes, always replace it.
- "The item is behind the counter (tobacco/electronics)." → Don't place it; give those tags to the manager at the end of your shift.
- "I can't find the item." → Use the Item Finder to see the product photo; if still stuck after ~2 minutes, skip and come back.
- "Do I have to scan every tag?" → No. Scanning is a help tool, not a requirement.
- "The name on my sheet doesn't match the app." → Expected — names can differ slightly. Confirm with the SKU/UPC last 4.

## 9. Learning moments (the skills taught in detail)

### 9.1 Finding your section
- DG shelves are broken into **4-foot sections**, each with a letter (A, B, C…).
- Once you find the aisle, **look at the bottom right of the shelf tags** to find the section letter.
- **Validate** you're in the right place by scanning product tags within that 4-foot area (collect 3 positive scans).

### 9.2 Working a shelf & removing expired product (Product Rotation)
- **Work the full section:** "Always pull products from the entire 4-foot section, working top to bottom until all items are reviewed. Don't rush or skip rows."
- **Pull product:** "Pull every product off the shelf and put it on the cart. Remember to pull every item off the shelf!"
- **Remove expired products:** "Check expiration dates. Set expired items to the side. Place other items back on the shelf." Rule: **remove items that expire on or before next month** (the task list gives the exact cutoff month).
- **Practice:** review photos and decide if each item is expired or can go back.

### 9.3 Placing items back — FIFO
- "Put non-expired items back on the shelf in **FIFO order — soonest expiration dates in front**."
- Why: "You want the product that expires soonest to be at the front of the row for customers to buy first."

### 9.4 Refilling empty facings
- Grab product from the **top of the shelf** (top stock) — no backroom trip needed.
- Read shelf tags to see where products go; restock empty facings if there's room, keeping dates ordered; return anything that doesn't fit to the top.

### 9.5 Reading the price tag sheet (Price Tag Updates) — the core skill
The sheet tells you **which aisle, section, and row** each tag belongs to. Steps:
1. **Tags are grouped by Planogram.** The sheet color-groups tags by category (e.g. Cleaning Wipes, Laundry) to keep you organized. **Planogram Labels are the yellow tags without prices.**
2. **Planogram name → aisle (use it as a clue).** "The Planogram name won't always match up to an aisle name." Example: "Insect & Rodent Control" might be in "Cleaning" or "Outdoor." *Tip: "If you can't find an exact match, try an aisle that is a similar category."*
3. **Find the correct section.** "The section you're looking for is on the **bottom right** of the tag" (e.g. the "A" in an "A-3" badge).
4. **Find the correct row.** "The number next to the aisle is the **row** the item is in" (the "3" in "A-3"). Rows count **from the bottom up**.
5. **Find the correct item.** Confirm the **last 4 digits of the UPC match** on both the sheet tag and the shelf tag.
6. **Change out the tag.** "Pull back the plastic sleeve and put the **new tag OVER the old tag**."

**Worked aisle examples (planogram label → aisle):**
- "Lysol Disinfct Wipes" → **Cleaning**
- "Tide Liquid Laundry" → **Laundry Detergent**
- "Insect & Rodent Control" → **Cleaning**

## 11. Using the Shiftsmart scanner

- **Open it** from your task list — tap the blue scan button. A counter shows progress (e.g. "0 of 3 products scanned").
- **Scan each product** — a product photo shows with its barcode highlighted; tap "Tap to scan." The counter advances and the next product loads. Use the arrow to skip and come back.
- **Submit** — once everything in the section is scanned, the Submit button activates. Submitting sends scans to DG, which they use to track stock and reorder.
- **Closed it early?** Your scans for that section are still saved — reopen from the task list to finish.

## 12. "Why it matters" — rationale the concierge can give

- **Scanning expired items:** "Scanning expired products helps you get paid within 2 hours," and it lets "Dollar General know which items are expired."
- **Before/after & cart photos:** "We use the photos to confirm work completion. Take all photos to get paid within 2 hours!" Photos prove you spent at least an hour on the section, worked every shelf, and pulled backstock.
- **Scanning empty facings:** "This helps Dollar General keep items in stock!"
- **FIFO:** Soonest-to-expire product up front means customers buy it first and less product expires on the shelf.

## 13. Quick-reference cheat sheets

**Before you go (every shift):**
- Wear: closed-toe shoes/boots, jeans/pants, neutral collared shirt.
- Leave bags/backpacks out of the store.
- Be kind and professional with everyone.
- Have your digital badge/LOA ready.

**Product Rotation in one line:** Check in → find & scan-confirm your section → before photo → pull every item, remove anything expiring this month or earlier, restock the rest FIFO → refill empty facings from top stock → after photo → scan empties → repeat → validate cart, photo + scan + log expired, hand off to manager, return equipment.

**Price Tag Updates in one line:** Check in → count & scan your sheets → for each tag use the planogram label to find the aisle, read section (bottom-right) and row (count from bottom), match the last 4 of the UPC, slide the new tag over the old → validate: scan & report any leftover tags with a reason.

**Price tag special cases:** EC = end cap (check both ends) · SW = sidewing · SF = specialty fixture · P = peg board (no section/row labels — search) · behind-the-counter tags (tobacco/small electronics) go to the manager at end of shift, never placed · replace identical-looking tags anyway.

**Numbers to remember:** ~1 hour per 4-foot section · up to 4 sections per shift · each aisle will have 8-12 sections  · find ≥10 expired items · 3 positive scans to confirm a section · paid within ~2 hours when fully logged · skip anything you can't find within ~2 minutes and come back.

**Golden rules:** You're a vendor, not a DG employee · only do the shift you signed up for · talk to the manager only at check-in and check-out · send customers to a DG employee.
