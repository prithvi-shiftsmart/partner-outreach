# Backup & Floater Shifts — Source of Truth

## What Is a Backup Shift?

A backup shift is a standby assignment. The partner shows up at the store at the scheduled time, and one of two things happens:

1. **Primary partner doesn't check in by shift start** → Backup gets **promoted to primary** and works the full shift with the full task list. The original primary's assignment is canceled.
2. **Primary partner checks in on time** → Backup is **dismissed** (released). The partner gets paid for showing up.

Backup shifts show a **"Backup"** badge on the shift card in the app.

## What Is a Floater Shift?

A floater shift is similar — the partner is on standby at the store in case extra coverage is needed. If the store doesn't need extra help, the partner is auto-dismissed after a waiting period (typically 30–60 minutes after check-in).

## How Promotion Works (Backup)

- Promotion happens **automatically at the primary's shift start time**.
- If the primary hasn't checked in by that exact moment, the backup is promoted.
- A **push notification** says "You've been promoted to primary partner."
- Once promoted, open the shift in the app and start the task list immediately.

**Key facts for partner communication:**
- Promotion comes as a **push notification only** — there is no phone call or text message sent.
- If notifications are off, the partner needs to check the app directly.
- The in-app shift details screen shows a banner: "You've been promoted to primary partner. Please start your shift immediately."

## How Dismissal Works (Backup)

- If the primary checks in on time, the backup is dismissed.
- **There is currently no automatic notification** (no call, text, or push) when a backup is dismissed.
- The partner should check the app for their shift status, or ask the store manager whether the primary has arrived.

## How Dismissal Works (Floater)

- Floaters are auto-dismissed after a configurable idle period (typically 30–60 minutes after check-in).
- Operators can also dismiss floaters manually.

## Pay for Backup and Floater Shifts

- Partners **get paid for showing up**, even if the primary arrives and they're dismissed.
- The exact amount depends on the shift's pay terms — a pay markdown may apply for dismissed shifts.
- Payment processes the same way as a regular shift — check the **Earnings tab** in the app.
- If payment doesn't appear or looks incorrect: go to completed shifts in the app and tap **"Report an Issue"**.

## "Payment at Risk" After a Backup Shift

The task list on a backup shift shows the same full task list as a regular shift. If the backup is dismissed before completing those tasks, the app may show a **"payment at risk"** warning. This is a known system behavior — it does not mean the partner won't be paid.

**What to tell the partner**: Go to completed shifts in the app and tap **"Report an Issue"** — the team will review and make sure payment is processed correctly.

## What to Do Day-of as a Backup

1. Show up at the store at the scheduled time
2. Check in via the app (geofence required — must be at the store)
3. Wait and check the app for status updates:
   - If promoted → start the task list immediately
   - If the primary is already there → check the app to confirm dismissal, then leave
4. If unsure about status after shift start time, ask the store manager whether the primary has arrived

## DO NOT QUOTE

- Don't say "you'll receive a call or text" when promoted or dismissed — promotion is push notification only, dismissal currently has no notification
- Don't promise a specific payment amount for dismissed backups — payment terms vary
- Don't promise promotion will happen at a specific time beyond "at the shift start time"
- Don't tell partners to wait outside indefinitely — if status is unclear after shift start, check the app and ask the store manager
- Don't explain internal system mechanics (auto-promote job, dismissal flags, pay markdown percentages)
- Don't say "payment at risk means you won't be paid" — it's a system artifact on backup shifts
