# First Shift Expectations — Source of Truth

## What to Expect

- No one expects perfection on day one — come in with a learning mindset
- The Shiftsmart app will guide you through every step of your shift via the task list. The manager on duty is there if you need help, but the app is your primary guide.
- "Done well" looks like: showed up on time, followed the task list in the app, asked questions when unsure

## Extra Time on First Two Shifts

- First shift includes a built-in **+30 minute extension** — it's there so partners can settle in and learn without rushing
- Second shift includes a built-in **+15 minute extension**
- The extension shows as a pop-up in the app when you check in, and again right before your original check-out time — you'll also see a "+30 min – You have extra time today" banner at the top of your task list
- This is automatic for every new partner — no action needed ahead of time

## What Success Looks Like

- Show up on time (or a few minutes early)
- Follow the task list in the app step by step — the app is your guide for every action during the shift
- Ask the manager on duty if something is unclear (but consult the task list first)
- Check out via the app when done
- Complete the entire task list before you leave the store
- Log everything — including what you didn't cook and why

## Golden Rules

1. Follow the task list — it is your guide for every step
2. Cook it, package it, label it, scan it, log it — always in that order
3. Log everything — including what you didn't cook and why (this is what gets you paid quickly)
4. Be respectful to the store team and the manager
5. Wear your gloves and hairnet the whole time
6. Finish the full task list before you leave

## Shift Duration

Shifts are usually 2–4 hours. Common types: Breakfast cook (4–5 AM), Lunch cook (8–9 AM), Snack cook (12–1 PM), Bin Prep (flexible PM hours).

## After Your Shift

- Payment processes via direct deposit — Shiftsmart releases it same day; your bank (via Stripe) typically takes 1–2 business days to deposit
- If anything went wrong during the shift, go to completed shifts in the app and tap "Report an Issue"

## DO NOT QUOTE

- Don't promise specific performance benchmarks ("you'll do great", "you'll finish in X minutes")
- Don't say the shift is "easy" or "simple" — it minimizes what partners are stepping into
- Don't speculate about store-specific routines or equipment beyond what's in the task list
- Don't quote specific shift times — they vary by store and market
