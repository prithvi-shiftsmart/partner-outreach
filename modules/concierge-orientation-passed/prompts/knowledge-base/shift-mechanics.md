# Shift Mechanics — Source of Truth

## Clocking In and Out
- Check in via the Shiftsmart app when you arrive — geofence required, must be at the store
- Check in within 10 minutes of your shift start time — later than that hurts your On Time Rate
- You can clock out whenever you want — you're only paid for the time you're checked in
- If you're more than 20 minutes late to the start of your shift, you may be removed from the shift

## On Time Rate
- Based on how often you check in within 10 minutes of shift start
- Higher On Time Rate = better access to shifts and opportunities

## Reliability Score
- Based on: (Accepted Shifts - No Shows - Late Cancels) / Accepted Shifts
- Higher score = priority access to better shifts, new project opportunities, and free or discounted Instant Pay
- No-shows have the most severe impact
- Same-day cancellations also hurt your score
- Cancel at least 24 hours before your shift to minimize impact

## Shift Duration
- Shifts are usually 2–4 hours
- Common shift types: Breakfast cook (4–5 AM), Lunch cook (8–9 AM), Snack cook (12–1 PM), Bin Prep (flexible PM hours)

## Backup Shift Mechanics

Some shifts are **backup** assignments — you're on standby in case the primary partner doesn't show.

### What Happens at Shift Start
- At the primary's shift start time, the system checks whether the primary checked in.
- **Primary checked in** → backup is dismissed (released). You get paid for showing up.
- **Primary didn't check in** → backup is promoted to primary. You work the full shift.

### Promotion
- You'll receive a **push notification**: "You've been promoted to primary partner."
- No phone call or text is sent — check the app directly if you have notifications off.
- Once promoted, open the shift and start the task list immediately.

### Dismissal / Release
- There is **no automatic notification** when you're released. Check the app for your shift status.
- If unsure, ask the store manager whether the primary has arrived.
- You get paid for showing up — check the **Earnings tab** for payment.

### Payment at Risk Warning
If you see "payment at risk" after a backup shift, it's because the task list showed tasks you weren't expected to complete. Go to completed shifts → **"Report an Issue"** and the team will make sure you're paid correctly.

## DO NOT QUOTE
- Don't quote specific On Time Rate or Reliability Score thresholds
- Don't say "you'll be fine if you're a few minutes late" — 10 minutes is the cutoff
- Don't promise specific shift durations — they vary
- Don't say "you'll receive a call or text" for backup promotion or dismissal
- Don't promise specific payment amounts for dismissed backups
