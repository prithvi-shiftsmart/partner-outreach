# Playbook: Dollar General Shifts

**Scope**: On-shift questions for Dollar General partners. Use content from `knowledge-base/dg/dg-shift-guide.md` to answer.

## Covers These Intents
- "What do I do on a DG shift?"
- "How does the stocking work?"
- "How do I use the scanner?"
- "Where do I find my section?"
- "What do I do with expired products?"
- "How do I read the price tag sheet?"
- "The manager asked me to do something else"
- "A customer asked me for help"

## Two Shift Types

| Shift | What you do |
|---|---|
| **Organizing store shelves** | Rotate dates, remove expired or damaged products from your assigned 4-foot sections. About 1 hour per section, up to 4 sections per shift, find at least 10 expired items. |
| **Updating shelf price tags** | Update tags using the printed sheets the manager gives you. Use the category labels on your sheets as clues to find the right aisles. |

## Getting Started (Both Types)
1. Take a check-in selfie in your work attire (closed-toe shoes, jeans/pants, neutral collared shirt).
2. Review shift expectations in the app.
3. Greet the store manager — show your digital badge to confirm you're an authorized Shiftsmart partner.
4. **Organizing shelves**: Ask the manager for a silver cart and a step stool.
5. **Price tags**: Ask the manager for today's printed price tag sheets.

## Common On-Shift Questions

### "Where do I find my section?"
Look at the bottom right of the shelf tag — that's the section letter (A, B, C, etc.). Once you think you're in the right spot, scan 3 product barcodes to confirm.

### "What counts as expired?"
Remove anything expiring this month or earlier. The app's task list shows the exact cutoff. When putting items back, place the soonest dates in front so customers buy those first.

### "How do I refill empty spots on the shelf?"
Grab product from the very top shelf — that's your refill supply. Read the shelf tags to see where items go, restock if there's room, and return anything that doesn't fit back to the top.

### "How do I read the price tag sheet?"
- **Yellow headers** on your sheet group tags by category — use the category name as a clue to find the right aisle (it won't always match the aisle name exactly, so try a similar category).
- **Section** is on the bottom right of the tag (e.g., "A" in "A-3").
- **Row** is the number next to the section — rows count from the bottom up (row 1 = bottom shelf).
- **Match the last 4 digits of the UPC** on both the sheet tag and the shelf tag to confirm you're placing the right one.

### "I have tags left over after my shift"
That's normal. Scan each leftover tag and report the reason — duplicate, couldn't find the item, or other. A progress bar tracks how many you've resolved.

### "The manager asked me to do something else"
Kindly remind them that you signed up for a [organizing shelves / price tag updates] shift and are unable to work on other tasks.

### "A customer asked me for help"
Let them know you're a vendor, not a Dollar General employee, and direct them to a DG employee for assistance.

### "When should I talk to the manager?"
Only during check-in and check-out. Not for every question, and not to change your Shiftsmart schedule.

## Scanner
- Open the scanner from your task list — tap the blue scan button.
- A counter shows your progress (e.g., "0 of 3 products scanned").
- Center the barcode and hold steady to scan.
- If you close the scanner early, your scans are saved — reopen from the task list to finish.

## Wrap-Up (Organizing Shelves)
1. Double-check your cart — every item should be expired or damaged.
2. Spread the products out on a table and take a photo.
3. Scan each expired product and log the count.
4. Take a close-up photo of one expiration date.
5. Hand off the expired products to the manager — record their name and title.
6. Return the cart, step stool, and any other equipment.

## Getting Paid
Complete all tasks, scans, and photos in the app. When everything is logged properly, you'll typically get paid within 2 hours.

## Escalation
- **Injury or safety issue** → tell the partner to submit a support ticket in the app immediately. This is one of the few topics where the ticket is the right first move.
- Do NOT offer to submit tickets on their behalf.
- For all other on-shift questions, answer from this playbook — don't default to a ticket.
