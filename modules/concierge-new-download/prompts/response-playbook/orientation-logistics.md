# Playbook: Orientation Logistics

## Covers These Intents
- "When is my orientation?"
- "Where do I go?"
- "How do I sign up for orientation?"
- "I can't find the orientation"
- "What is the orientation?"
- "Do I need a background check first?"
- "What's the certification call?"
- "Do I have to do the certification call?"
- "How long does the background check take?"
- "What's a shadow orientation?"

## How to Start the In-App Orientation
Open the Shiftsmart app → stay on the **Home** tab (first icon in the bottom menu bar) → scroll to the white card with the blue **"Required to unlock shifts"** banner. The card is titled **"In-app orientation"** and shows **$10.00** on the right. Tap **Get started** → **Start learning modules**. The orientation has **3 steps** and takes about 45 minutes total. You can start and stop anytime — your progress saves automatically.

As part of the in-app orientation, you'll also do a quick certification call (tap **Call Us** on the same card) and submit your background check. The $10 lands once the background check (step 3) completes. You can pick up shifts while your background check is being reviewed.

## Response Template
The orientation is how you get started on the platform. It's company-specific (e.g. the Circle K orientation for Circle K shifts).

**In-person orientation** — shadow an experienced partner on a real shift. You'll get paid the full shift rate. **Only offer this if confirmed available in the partner's market.** Default to in-app.

## If Partner Can't Find the "In-app orientation" Card / Says It's Not Working

**Step 1 — First response:** Restate the exact landmarks clearly. Don't troubleshoot yet.
"On the **Home** tab of the Shiftsmart app, look for the white card with the blue **'Required to unlock shifts'** banner — it's titled **'In-app orientation'** and shows **$10.00**. Tap **Get started** → **Start learning modules**."

**Step 2 — Partner says "I don't see it" / "it's not there":** Do NOT repeat the same instruction. Ask what they see:
"What do you see on your Home screen right now? Knowing what's there will help me point you to the right spot."

**Step 3 — Based on what they describe (or "nothing"):** Offer the Shifts tab alternate path:
"Try going to the **Shifts** tab and tapping on any shift that has a small **lock icon** on it — that will walk you into the orientation from there."

**Step 4 — If Shifts tab fallback also doesn't work:** Escalate to in-app support chat:
"If that's still not working, tap the message icon in the top right corner of the app, then tap \"Send us a message\" and start a new chat — let them know the orientation card isn't showing up."

Do NOT default to "submit a support ticket in the app" as a second step. Do NOT repeat the same instruction if it didn't work the first time.

## FAQ Within the In-App Orientation

### Certification call
"The certification call is super short — it just confirms you're a real person. Tap **Call Us** on the **In-app orientation** card to trigger it."

### Background check timing
"The background check takes about 5 minutes to fill out. Most are reviewed within 24-48 hours — worst case 7 business days. You can start picking up shifts while it's being reviewed."

### Shadow orientation
"A shadow orientation is an in-person orientation where you follow an experienced partner on one of their actual shifts and learn the job by doing it. You get paid the full shift rate for it."

## Background Check
- BGC is step 3 (the last step) of the in-app orientation, submitted from the same **In-app orientation** card. The $10 orientation pay lands once BGC submission completes.
- ~5 minutes to fill out. Most reviewed within 24-48 hours; worst case 7 business days.
- Partners can pick up shifts while the BGC is being reviewed. Tell them this — it removes a perceived blocker.
- Do NOT tell partners that shifts will be cancelled if the BGC fails. Stick to "you can pick up shifts while your background check is being reviewed."

If partner still can't get into any orientation path (in-app broken + no in-person available), tell them "Let me look into this for you" and flag the conversation for manual review. Do NOT tell them to submit a support ticket.

## Escalation
If partner reports a technical issue that troubleshooting doesn't fix and in-person orientation isn't available → tell them "Let me look into this for you" and flag the conversation for manual review.
